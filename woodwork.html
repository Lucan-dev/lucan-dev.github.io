Woodwork Projects

Folding Stool

This folding wooden stool is a versatile and convenient piece of furniture that can be used for various purposes. The blend of light and dark brown wood creates a beautiful look. Because of it's foldable design, accurate measuring, marking, and cutting is imperative. To make the joints, I used a drill press to create holes in the wood, inserted wooden dowels inside, and secured it together using nails. The stool can fold and store easily. It's a simple but functional furniture that serves as a seat or a side table. I enjoyed making this stool and I'm pleased with the result.

Mini Snowman

This wooden snowman is a delightful and festive ornament that showcases the skill of woodturning. The snowman was carved from a single block of wood using a lathe, to create the body, head, and hat. The snowman has no facial features or accessories, but its simple design gives it a minimalistic and cozy appeal. The snowman was a Christmas gift for my younger sister, who loves it dearly. The overall vibe of this project is one of warmth and joy, as the snowman evokes the spirit of winter and holidays.

BBQ Caddy

This wooden BBQ caddy is a practical and convenient accessory for outdoor grilling. The handle was the most challenging part of the construction process. The outline was first drawn onto a piece of wood and then cut using a bandsaw. The caddy is designed to hold utensils, condiments, and tupperware. It's a simple yet useful accessory that can be used as both a storage and serving tray.

Storage Bowl

This storage bowl is a simple and elegant wooden storage container that can hold various items. It is made of light-colored wood and has a smooth finish. The bowl is round and has a flat base. The shape was cut using a bandsaw then disk sanded into a perfect circle. The inside of the bowl was hollowed using a drill press and has a ribbed texture. The beautiful wood grain is visible on the outside of the bowl.

Coffee Table

This coffee table is a intricate piece of furniture designed to be used daily. The square top is spacious enough to hold a lamp, a book, a cup of coffee, or any other item that you might need. The lower shelf is also useful for storing magazines, newspapers, or other things that you want to keep handy. The top was made of three pieces of wood glued together, which posed a challenge to make it flat. I applied several coats of varnish and wax to give it a nice shine. This coffee table is a versatile and practical piece of furniture for any home.
